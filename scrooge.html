Scrooge

FK/IK System · Non-Roll · Stretch · Blend Shapes · Partial Joints · Push Joints

See on Vimeo

3D model from Alvirenz-101, CGTrader.

This full character rig was designed to provide animators with precise control and intuitive usability, making it suitable for a wide range of animation workflows.



Facial Rig

The facial rig includes a set of dedicated controls designed to give animators greater precision over expressions. Additionally, an FK chain was created for the hair lock on the forehead and two lattice deformers were implemented: one to simulate jaw movement during chewing and another to control the deformation of the remaining hair.

See on Vimeo

To achieve this facial rig, the following process has been followed:

  • Blocking (first image): initial weight assignment was applied, binding each group of vertices to the appropriate joint.

  • Smooth (second image): after blocking, the weights were refined and smoothed between adjacent joints to improve deformation.
  • Correctives (third image): finally, different Blend Shapes and Set Driven Keys have been used to achieve better expressions in the character and make it look more realistic.

Body Rig

The body rig includes multiple follow options (for the head, shoulders, neck, clavicles, feet, etc.) to give animators greater flexibility while posing and animating the character. In addition to this, a Non-Roll System and a Stretch System have also been implemented for both arms and legs, and Partial Joints and Push Joints have been used to achieve a better deformation.

See on Vimeo

To obtain this body rig, the following process has been followed:

  • Blocking (first image): initial weight painting was applied by assigning vertices to their corresponding joints.

  • Smooth (second image): after blocking, the weights were smoothed to distribute deformation more naturally between adjacent joints.

  • Correctives (third image): finally, Partial Joints and Push Joints have been created for the shoulders, elbows, wrists, neck, hips and knees and Blend Shapes have been used to correct the deformation and obtain a better result in the body.

Clothes

For the character's clothing, several FK chains were created (such as for the shirt collar), along with a hybrid FK-IK system for the lower part of the dressing gown. These setups provide animators with more control and help achieve realistic movement without relying on simulation or physics.
