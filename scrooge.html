Scrooge

IK/FK System - Non Roll - Stretch - Blend Shapes - Partial Joints - Push Joints

3D model from Alvirenz-101, CGTrader.



Facial Rig

Specific controls have been included in the facial rig with the aim of providing greater control to the animator. In addition to this, a FK chain has been created for the lock of hair on the forehead and two Lattice-type deformers have been used: one of them to simulate the movement of the jaw when chewing and another to deform the rest of the hair.


To achieve this facial rig, the following process has been followed:

  • Blocking (first image): in this stage, the weights of the vertexes of the mesh have been given to the corresponding bone.

  • Smooth (second image): once the blocking of the face has been done, the weights have been distributed among the adjacent bones using the smooth in order to achieve a better result.

  • Correctives (third image): finally, different Blend Shapes and Set Driven Keys have been used to achieve better expressions in the character and make it look more realistic.

Body Rig

In the body rig, multiple follows have been created (for the head, shoulders, neck, clavicles, feet, etc.) to give the animator more freedom when animating. In addition to this, a Non-Roll System and a Stretch System have also been implemented for both arms and legs, and Partial Joints and Push Joints have been used to achieve a better deformation.


To obtain this body rig, the following process has been followed:

  • Blocking (first image): in this stage, each bone has been given the weight of the corresponding vertexes.

  • Smooth (second image): after blocking, smooth has been used in order to distribute the weights between the adjacent bones and obtain a better result in the deformation of the mesh.

  • Correctives (third image): finally, Partial Joints and Push Joints have been created for the shoulders, elbows, wrists, neck, hips and knees and Blend Shapes have been used to correct the deformation and obtain a better result in the body.

Clothes

In the case of the character's clothing, a series of FK chains (for the collar of the shirt) and a hybrid FK-IK system (for the lower part of the dressing gown) have been created in order to give the animator more freedom and to achieve a more realistic result without the need for physics in the clothing.
